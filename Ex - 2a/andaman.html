Andaman and Nicobar Islands

  • Area: The Andaman and Nicobar Islands cover approximately 8,249 square kilometers.
  • Population: As per the 2011 census, the population was around 380,500 people.
  • Capital: Port Blair is the capital city and the largest town in the Andaman and Nicobar Islands.
  • GDP: The Gross State Domestic Product (GSDP) of the Andaman and Nicobar Islands is not readily available due to its smaller scale and unique administrative status. The economy is largely dependent on tourism, fisheries, and agriculture.
  • Economy: Tourism is a major economic driver, with the islands known for their pristine beaches, coral reefs, and marine biodiversity. Fisheries, coconut farming, and government employment are also significant contributors to the local economy.
  • Literacy Rate: The literacy rate in the Andaman and Nicobar Islands was around 86.63% as per the 2011 census, reflecting relatively high educational attainment levels.
  • Languages: Hindi, Bengali, Tamil, Telugu, and Nicobarese languages are widely spoken due to the diverse population comprising mainland settlers and indigenous communities.
  • Tourism: The islands attract tourists for activities like scuba diving, snorkeling, and exploring historical sites such as Cellular Jail in Port Blair. The pristine natural beauty and ecological diversity make it a unique destination.
